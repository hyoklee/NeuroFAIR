## [2026-05-14] fix | mpi4py rebuilt against Aurora MPICH — MPI_Comm_dup SIGSEGV root cause resolved

Root cause of the Case 6 `run-network` rank-0 SIGSEGV confirmed as two different MPI libraries loaded in the same process: `mpi4py`'s pip-wheel binary was linked against conda OpenMPI 5.0 (`libmpi.so.40`), while `neuroh5/io.so` was linked against Aurora MPICH 5.0 (`libmpi.so.12`). The SIGSEGV in `MPI_Comm_dup` occurred because the two libraries have incompatible `MPI_Comm` struct layouts. Fix: rebuilt `mpi4py` 4.1.1 `MPI.cpython-312-x86_64-linux-gnu.so` from source against Aurora MPICH 5.0.0 using the system `mpicc`. Required temporarily hiding the conda OpenMPI headers and libraries during the build (both `~/miniconda3/envs/miv/include/mpi.h` and `lib/libmpi.so.40*`) so the linker picked up Aurora MPICH's `libmpi.so.12` instead. Also cleared ~3.4 GB pip cache to recover `ddn_ssd` pool quota (was at 54 GB against a 50 GB soft / 55 GB hard limit). The rebuilt `.so` has 0 `ompi_*` symbols and `ldd` confirms `libmpi.so.12 => /opt/aurora/.../mpich-5.0.0.aurora_test.../lib/libmpi.so.12`. Submitted PBS 8484211 (`miv_case6_perf.pbs`) to `debug-scaling` to re-run the 4-condition sweep (A: CPU+Lustre, B: CPU+/dev/shm, C: Intel-GPU+Lustre, D: Intel-GPU+/dev/shm, `tstop=100 ms`).
